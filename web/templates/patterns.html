Trading Metrics & Signals Explained
üìä Chart Controls
  • Symbol: Stock ticker (e.g., AAPL, TSLA) - identifies which stock to analyze
  • Timeframe: Candle duration (1m, 5m, 1h, 1d) - shorter = more detail, longer = bigger picture
  • Period: How much history to load (1d to 2y) - more data = better patterns
  • Live Toggle: Auto-refresh every 5 seconds to show latest price movements
üìà Technical Indicators
  • EMA 20/50: Moving averages showing trend direction and strength
  • RSI 14: Momentum oscillator (0-100) - below 30 = oversold, above 70 = overbought
  • MACD: Momentum indicator showing trend changes via line crossovers
  • Bollinger Bands: Volatility bands - price touching bands = potential reversal
  • Volume: Trading activity - higher volume = stronger signal confirmation
Trading Signals Explained
üéØ Signal Types
  • Bullish Trend: Price > EMA20 > EMA50 - strong uptrend
  • Bearish Trend: Price < EMA20 < EMA50 - strong downtrend
  • RSI Entry Zone: RSI 30-70 - optimal entry timing
  • RSI Oversold: RSI < 30 - strong buy signal
  • RSI Overbought: RSI > 70 - strong sell signal
  • MACD Bullish: MACD line above signal line - momentum up
  • MACD Bearish: MACD line below signal line - momentum down
  • BB Breakout: Price breaks upper band with volume - continuation up
  • BB Breakdown: Price breaks lower band with volume - continuation down
üí∞ Trade Parameters
  • Entry: Exact price to buy/sell the stock
  • Stop Loss: Price to exit if trade goes against you (risk control)
  • Target: Price to take profits (reward goal)
  • R:R (Risk:Reward): Ratio of potential profit to potential loss (2:1 = good)
  • Confidence: Signal reliability percentage (70%+ = high confidence)
üìä Pattern Recognition
  • Double Bottom: Two equal lows - bullish reversal pattern
  • Double Top: Two equal highs - bearish reversal pattern
  • Head & Shoulders: Three peaks (middle highest) - bearish reversal
  • Bull Flag: Sharp rally + shallow pullback - continuation up
  • Bear Flag: Sharp decline + weak bounce - continuation down
  • Triangle: Converging support/resistance - breakout coming
Risk Analysis & Position Sizing
‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Metrics
  • ATR (Average True Range): Measures recent price volatility in dollars - higher ATR = more volatile stock
  • Volatility: ATR as percentage of price - shows how much stock moves daily
  • Position Size: Number of shares to buy based on 1-2% risk rule
  • Stop Distance: How far to place stop loss (usually 2x ATR)
  • Risk per Trade: Maximum capital to risk on this trade (1-2% recommended)
üìà Key Statistics
  • Last Price: Current stock price at market close
  • Daily Change: Price change from previous day (positive = up, negative = down)
  • Volume: Number of shares traded (M = millions, B = billions)
  • High Volume: Above average trading activity - confirms signal strength
  • Low Volume: Below average activity - signal may be weak
Swing Trading Rules & Best Practices
üéØ Entry Rules
  • Wait for Confirmation: Don't enter until pattern is complete
  • Volume Confirmation: Higher volume on breakout = stronger signal
  • Multiple Timeframes: Check higher timeframe for overall trend
  • Support/Resistance: Key levels must be respected
  • Risk First: Always know your stop loss before entering
üõ°Ô∏è Risk Management
  • 1-2% Rule: Never risk more than 1-2% of account per trade
  • Stop Losses: Always use stops - never hope for recovery
  • Position Sizing: Calculate shares based on stop distance
  • Take Profits: Target 2:1 or 3:1 risk-reward ratio
  • Max Drawdown: Never risk more than 6% total account
üìö Learning Tips
  • Paper Trade First: Practice without real money
  • Keep Records: Track all trades and outcomes
  • Start Small: Use small position sizes while learning
  • Be Patient: Wait for high-probability setups
  • Stay Disciplined: Follow your rules consistently
Swing Trading Rules & Risk Management
Entry Rules
  • Pattern Completion: Wait for clear pattern formation
  • Volume Confirmation: Higher volume on breakout/breakdown
  • Support/Resistance: Key levels must be respected
  • Trend Alignment: Pattern should align with overall trend

  • Stop Loss: Place below/above pattern levels
  • Take Profit: Target 2:1 or 3:1 risk-reward ratio
  • Trailing Stops: Move stops to breakeven after 1:1
